### Key Features of Codeverta Coffee Shop ERP

Here are the core features that make this system useful for coffee shop operations:

- **Automatic Recipe and COGS Management**  
  Calculate the cost of goods sold for every cup based on coffee beans, milk, syrup, toppings, and other ingredients. Stock is deducted automatically when a transaction happens.

- **Fast and Intuitive Point of Sale (POS)**  
  A cashier interface designed for baristas, tablets, and desktop devices. Orders can be processed faster with modifiers such as extra shot, sugar level, cup size, and add-ons.

- **Real-Time Kitchen Display System (KDS)**  
  Orders appear directly on the kitchen or bar screen in sequence, reducing mistakes and speeding up production.

- **Real-Time Inventory and Stock Alerts**  
  Track coffee beans, milk, cups, and other supplies accurately. Get automatic notifications when stock is running low.

- **Loyalty Program and Customer CRM**  
  Support digital points, memberships, and customer preference tracking to improve retention and personalize promotions.

- **Multi-Branch Sales Reports**  
  Analyze best-selling products, peak-hour performance, and profitability across outlets from a single dashboard.

- **QR Ordering System**  
  Customers can order and pay directly from their table without waiting in line, which is especially useful during busy hours.

### Tutorial: How to Use Codeverta Coffee Shop ERP Step by Step

Here is a practical implementation flow:

**Step 1: Initial System Setup**  
Log in to the Codeverta dashboard, create your outlet account, and enter master data such as menu items, recipes, prices, and initial stock. For a small outlet, this usually takes less than one hour.

**Step 2: Configure Recipes and Inventory**  
Enter the ingredients for each menu item. For example, a latte may use 18 grams of coffee beans and 200 ml of milk. The system calculates COGS automatically and deducts inventory after every sale.

**Step 3: Activate POS and KDS**  
Install the cashier app on a tablet or desktop and set up the kitchen display. Run a few dummy transactions to make sure the workflow is correct.

**Step 4: Integrate Payments and QR Ordering**  
Connect the system to payment options such as QRIS or e-wallets, then print QR codes for each table.

**Step 5: Monitor and Analyze Performance**  
Open the dashboard every day to review sales, remaining stock, team performance, and peak hours. Set alerts based on your operational needs.

**Step 6: Launch a Loyalty Program**  
Create membership tiers and automatic points. Customers can receive rewards after specific transactions, increasing repeat visits.
