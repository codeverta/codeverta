# Why Kilo Laundry Businesses Often Miscalculate Orders and How to Fix It

Kilo laundry is attractive because the pricing model looks simple: weigh the clothes, multiply by the price per kilogram, and process the order. But in daily operations, mistakes happen easily.

Wrong weight, wrong service type, missing notes, unpaid add-ons, and unclear pickup status can quietly reduce profit.

## Common Causes of Miscalculation

### 1. Manual Weighing Notes

If weight is written manually, numbers can be misread or entered incorrectly.

### 2. Mixed Service Types

Wash-only, wash-and-iron, express service, bed cover, shoes, and special items often have different prices. Manual calculation increases the risk of error.

### 3. Missing Add-On Fees

Express fees, stain treatment, pickup delivery, and packaging may be forgotten.

### 4. Unclear Payment Status

Some customers pay upfront, some pay on pickup, and some owe payment. Without a system, staff can lose track.

### 5. No Order Status Tracking

If orders are not updated properly, staff may spend time searching for clothes that are already packed or picked up.

## How to Fix the Workflow

### Use Digital Transaction Records

Every order should record customer name, weight, service type, price, payment status, and pickup date.

### Standardize Service Packages

Define clear pricing for each service. Staff should not calculate manually every time.

### Print or Send Receipts

Receipts reduce disputes because customers can see weight, service, price, and estimated completion date.

### Track Order Status

Use statuses such as received, washing, drying, ironing, packed, ready, and picked up.

### Review Daily Reports

Owners should check daily revenue, unpaid orders, service volume, and cancelled orders.

## Benefits of a Laundry System

- Fewer pricing mistakes
- Faster cashier process
- Clearer payment status
- Less customer dispute
- Better order tracking
- More accurate profit reports

## Conclusion

Kilo laundry businesses often miscalculate not because the staff are careless, but because the workflow depends too much on manual records.

A simple laundry management system can make pricing, payment, and order tracking more accurate.
